# Research Methodology — High Success Rate Framework

This methodology is referenced by `Workflows/research-report.md`.
It is based on the **SIFT + Triangulation** approach used by investigative journalists and academic researchers.

---

## Core Principles

**1. Stop before you share**
Before accepting any source, pause and evaluate it — don't just search and quote.

**2. Investigate the source**
Look up who published it, when, and why. A blog post from a vendor is not the same as an independent study.

**3. Find better coverage**
If a claim seems important, don't stop at the first result. Find the original/primary source.

**4. Trace claims**
Follow links and citations back to their origin. Many "facts" online are copies of copies with the original context stripped.

---

## Search Strategy (5-Query Rule)

Run at least 5 searches per topic using varied approaches:

| Query Type | Example |
|---|---|
| Direct topic | "quantum computing applications 2024" |
| Expert opinion | "what experts say about quantum computing" |
| Counterargument | "quantum computing limitations problems" |
| Recent news | "quantum computing news 2024 2025" |
| Primary source | "IBM Google quantum computing research paper" |

---

## Source Quality Tiers

**Tier 1 — Prefer these:**
- Peer-reviewed papers, official documentation, government/institution reports
- Direct quotes from named domain experts
- Primary data (surveys, studies with methodology disclosed)

**Tier 2 — Use with attribution:**
- Reputable journalism (NYT, Reuters, BBC, Wired, etc.)
- Expert blog posts from verifiable practitioners
- Industry analyst reports (Gartner, McKinsey — note they may have bias)

**Tier 3 — Treat with skepticism:**
- Anonymous sources, undated content, vendor marketing, social media
- Use only to identify leads, not as standalone citations

---

## Triangulation Rule

Any claim marked as a key finding must be confirmed by **at least 2 independent sources** from Tier 1 or Tier 2.

If only one source supports a claim, label it: *"reported by [source], not independently verified."*

---

## Conflict Resolution

When sources contradict each other:
1. Note both positions explicitly — do not silently pick one
2. Identify which is more recent and/or from a higher-tier source
3. If unresolved, present as an open question: *"Views differ on this — [Source A] says X, [Source B] says Y."*

---

## Depth Guide

| Depth Level | Searches | Sources Read in Full | Expected Report Length |
|---|---|---|---|
| Surface summary | 5 | 2 | 300–500 words |
| Mid-depth analysis | 8 | 4–5 | 600–1000 words |
| Deep dive | 12+ | 7+ | 1200–2000 words |
